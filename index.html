The problem
30–50% of recommended work gets declined. Zero percent gets followed up.
Your tech found it. Your advisor recommended it. The customer said "not today." Then nothing happens. The work sits in your system and the revenue disappears.
📞

Your advisor can't make the calls

They're answering the phone, checking in walk-ins, ordering parts, and managing the counter. Following up on last month's declined work isn't happening — not because they don't care, but because there's no time.

💬

Generic reminders get ignored

"Time for your next service" texts are noise. They don't reference the specific work that was declined, don't explain why it matters, and don't give the customer a reason to act now.

The car still needs the work

Those brake pads are still at 2mm. That timing belt is still at 98k miles. The work is real, the need is real, and the customer just needs someone to remind them before it becomes an emergency — or they go somewhere else.

How it works
We make the follow-up your advisor can't.
1

We connect to your shop system

RecoverPro integrates with the platforms you already use — Tekmetric, Shop-Ware, Mitchell1, AutoFluent, Shopmonkey, and more. We pull every repair order and identify every declined job — what was recommended, the dollar amount, and the tech's notes on why it matters. Don't see your system? We're platform agnostic and can work with any shop management software.

2

AI sends a personal follow-up

Not a generic reminder. A message that references the customer's name, their specific vehicle, and the exact work that was declined — written in plain language, not mechanic jargon.

3

Text or phone call — we pick the right one

Standard maintenance gets a text. Safety-critical or high-value work gets an AI phone call that sounds like your best service advisor — warm, knowledgeable, and never pushy.

4

Customer replies → appointment booked

When the customer says yes, the system books the return visit. You don't lift a finger until the car shows up. The dashboard shows you exactly how much revenue came back.
